Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation in Bangladesh thrives through digital transformation, entrepreneurship, renewable energy, and tech startups, driving economic growth and societal progress.

Sustainability

Sustainability in Bangladesh focuses on renewable energy, climate resilience, sustainable agriculture, waste management, and equitable socio-economic development to ensure progress.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh's infrastructure is improving, with advancements in transportation, energy, and communication, but challenges remain in rural connectivity and urban congestion.

Empowerment

Empowerment in Bangladesh thrives through education, microfinance, and social reforms, fostering gender equality, entrepreneurship, and sustainable community development.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Let us reimagine our nation, not as a place of scarcity, but as a land of abundance—where every child goes to bed with a full stomach, where every young person has access to quality education, and where every family lives with dignity.

Padma Bridge: Transforming Bangladesh's Connectivity

Date: 05/01/2023 Time: 10:25 PM

Inaugurated in June 2022, the 6.15 km Padma Bridge links Dhaka with southwestern districts, enhancing trade and travel. A World Bank report anticipates a 1.23% GDP boost, with wages in connected regions rising by 2-4%. The bridge also facilitates quicker medical access, potentially saving lives.

Bangladesh Metro Rail: A New Era of Transportation

Date: 05/01/2024 Time: 10:25 PM

Bangladesh inaugurated its first metro rail, MRT Line-6, in December 2022, covering Uttara to Agargaon, later extended to Motijheel in November 2023. Spanning 21.26 km, it promises to ease Dhaka’s traffic. Additionally, the 17.20 km MRT Line-5 (Gabtoli-Dasherkandi) is set for completion by 2030, enhancing urban mobility.
